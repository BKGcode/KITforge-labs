# Product Opportunities — Initial Shortlist

Date: 2026-04-11

## Positioning rule

Do not build a giant all-in-one editor suite first.
Build a portfolio of focused products with clear day-to-day value.

## Wave 1 candidates

| Priority | Product lane | Why it fits the market | Main risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Project Health & Validation | Broad audience, strong safety value, easy demo | Crowded if too generic |
| 2 | Safe Cleanup & Dependency Map | Clear pain, strong before/after story | Must avoid false positives |
| 3 | Capture & Store Media Toolkit | Useful for indies and publishers, easy value proposition | Need polished UX |
| 4 | Inspector Productivity Pack | Always useful, high daily touch frequency | Heavy competition |
| 5 | Focused URP Micro FX Pack | Strong visuals, cross-sell potential | Higher support and pipeline variance |

## Recommended first direction

Start with **workflow safety** before visual flash.

Why:
- easier to differentiate with trust and clarity
- broader audience than niche graphics tools
- lower maintenance than large framework products
- strong commercial wording: save time, reduce mistakes, improve confidence

## Evaluation rubric for any new idea

Score each idea from 1-5 on:

- Pain intensity
- Audience size
- Setup simplicity
- Demo clarity
- Review risk
- Support burden
- Expansion potential

If an idea cannot explain its value in one sentence, it is not ready.

## Kill criteria

Avoid ideas that are:

- impressive but hard to explain
- deeply dependent on unstable Unity internals
- only useful for one niche team
- hard to support remotely
- impossible to demo clearly
